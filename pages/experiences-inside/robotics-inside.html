Featured experience

Autonomous Parking Assistant

I developed a parking assistant system designed to enhance autonomous vehicle functionality in urban environments by detecting and maneuvering into suitable parking spaces. The vehicle operates by driving parallel to parked cars while maintaining a fixed distance of 60 cm, continuously scanning and measuring potential spaces. When an adequately sized space is identified, the system evaluates whether parking is permitted by interpreting relevant traffic signs. It also incorporates obstacle detection in the direction of travel, ensuring safety by stopping the vehicle and waiting until the path is clear instead of attempting avoidance. This project demonstrates the integration of perception, decision-making, and control in an intelligent driver assistance system.

What I worked on

Parking space detection and measurement, distance control, traffic sign interpretation, obstacle detection logic, vehicle behavior planning.

Key skills

Sensor integration, real-time data processing, computer vision/traffic sign recognition, autonomous decision-making, control systems

Impact

Developed a reliable and safety-oriented parking assistant, improved understanding of autonomous driving systems, and demonstrated practical implementation of intelligent vehicle features

Nash Equilibrium in Autonomous Robotics

I developed a Nash equilibrium-based decision-making system within a Gazebo simulation environment to model and control autonomous agent behavior in dynamic scenarios. The project focused on implementing game-theoretic strategies that allow multiple agents to make optimal decisions by considering the actions and potential responses of others. By integrating the algorithm into Gazebo, I simulated realistic interactions between agents, enabling them to reach stable outcomes where no participant benefits from unilaterally changing their strategy. This work combined simulation, algorithm design, and multi-agent coordination to demonstrate intelligent and adaptive behavior in complex environments.

What I worked on

Game theory (Nash equilibrium), algorithm design, multi-agent systems, ROS/Gazebo simulation, decision-making under uncertainty

Key skills

Implementation of Nash equilibrium logic, agent interaction modeling, integration with Gazebo simulation, testing of strategic behaviors in dynamic environments

Impact

Demonstrated stable and optimal decision-making in multi-agent scenarios, enhanced understanding of strategic AI systems, and showcased the application of game theory in autonomous robotics simulations

SLAM implementation in simulation and real-world robotics

My bachelor’s thesis, which I obationed an upstanding award. It is focused on the development of a small scale autonomous vehicle capable of navigating unknown environments using SLAM. The project combined hardware integration and intelligent software design to enable real-time perception, mapping, and decision-making. Using a modified RC car platform, the system leveraged LiDAR based sensing and onboard processing with a Raspberry Pi to construct maps and localize itself without prior knowledge of the environment. The project explored how machine learning and robotic perception can enhance navigation accuracy and safety, contributing to the broader field of autonomous systems.

What I worked on

I designed and built an autonomous RC car platform from the ground up, integrating LiDAR sensors with a Raspberry Pi 4 for real-time data acquisition and perception. I implemented SLAM algorithms to enable mapping and localization in unknown environments, while also developing control systems for motor driving and Ackermann steering. Additionally, I managed the communication between the Arduino and Raspberry Pi, ensuring reliable hardware software interaction throughout the system.

Key skills

Throughout the project, I optimized power management and overall system performance to improve efficiency and stability. I conducted extensive testing to validate autonomous navigation in dynamic environments, strengthening my skills in robotics, SLAM, ROS 2 and simulation (Gazebo), sensor integration, embedded systems, control systems, and programming in Python and C++, along with problem-solving and system debugging.

Impact

This project demonstrates the practical implementation of autonomous navigation in a constrained, real-world system. It highlights the integration of mechanical design, electronics, and artificial intelligence to solve complex robotics problems. The work contributes to a deeper understanding of scalable autonomous solutions and showcases how low-cost platforms can be used to prototype advanced technologies relevant to self-driving vehicles, robotics, and smart mobility systems.
